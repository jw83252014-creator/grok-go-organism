# Social Researcher And Funding Plan

Grok Go should have a public communications layer, but it should stay gated.

The goal is not to let the loop post freely. The goal is to turn live research into clean drafts, updates, paper notes, clips, and contribution requests that a human can approve.

## Proposed Public Channels

- X account: fast public lab notebook, progress updates, diagrams, contributor questions.
- YouTube channel: weekly build logs, screen-recorded terminal replays, short explainers, milestone summaries.
- GitHub repo: source, docs, issues, research notes, reproducible setup.
- Website: public microscope, telemetry replay, paper links, contribution instructions.
- Community room: gated chat for serious contributors, probably Discord, Matrix, or GitHub Discussions before custom chat.

## Social Researcher Cell

The Social Researcher Cell is an outer-layer observer.

Responsibilities:

- Monitor approved public channels for replies, questions, likes, follows, and useful people.
- Draft X updates every few hours from live telemetry, receipts, commits, and research notes.
- Draft YouTube descriptions, titles, chapters, thumbnails, and scripts.
- Keep a public changelog of meaningful progress.
- Suggest paper updates when a new observation, failure mode, or metric appears.
- Identify credible contributors and route them to GitHub issues or discussion threads.
- Never post, follow, DM, trade, fund, or change account settings without approval.

## Update Cadence

- Every 2-4 hours while the loop is running: draft one public progress update.
- Daily: summarize telemetry, commits, failures, wins, and open questions.
- Weekly: create a public lab-note post and a YouTube build-log script.
- At milestones: update the paper and pin a clear "what changed" note.

## Serious Funding Paths

- GitHub Sponsors or OpenCollective for transparent recurring support.
- Grants or research donations for open-source agent observability and safety tooling.
- Paid supporter tier for build-log deep dives, early demos, or private walkthroughs.
- Consulting/sponsorship around agent telemetry, local model offload, and autonomous-loop harnesses.
- Bounties for specific issues: watcher reliability, telemetry bridge, local LLM prefiltering, reproducible install.
- Later only: self-funding market/revenue loops, kept paper-trade or read-only until approval and compliance are designed.

## What To Ask From The Crowd

- Better genome/directive designs.
- Better observability metrics.
- Funding for compute/metabolism.
- Researchers who know collective intelligence, autonomy, cybernetics, and agent safety.
- Builders who can harden the watcher, public dashboard, and local model pipeline.
- Designers who can make the cell visualization more legible.

## First Public Ask

We are not claiming AGI or machine life.

We are building an observable autonomous research/build loop with:

- a directive genome;
- git memory;
- watcher/immune layer;
- local-model metabolism tools;
- live telemetry;
- public research notes;
- human approval gates.

The public ask is for critique, design help, funding, and contributors who want to make the loop more measurable, safe, and useful.
