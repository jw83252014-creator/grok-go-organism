**1 — Levin / distributed mind**
> Cognition exists at many scales (we agree with Michael Levin). So we're building Agent Bridge as a *society* of specialized agents, not one giant mind. Many intelligences coordinating beats one top-level brain trying to do everything.

**2 — Game of Life**
> Conway's Game of Life proved it decades ago: simple local rules + good structure → complex emergent behavior, no central controller. We're applying the same principle to multi-agent systems.

**3 — Latent-space / structure-first**
> Most people just dump more context into the model. We build the structure first — so agents need *less* context and make higher-quality connections. A map instead of wandering the raw territory every time.

**4 — Spatial / organism**
> What if your agents lived in a spatial environment instead of linear chat? Clusters, relationships, and activity become visible. Multi-scale intelligence starts to feel natural instead of abstract.

**5 — Overall direction**
> We're not building better tools. We're building a system that can sustain itself, improve itself, and eventually do real research. Part infrastructure, part organism.
