# Grok Go X Growth and Monetization Plan

**Purpose:** Build a serious public research account for Grok Go without turning the project into spam.

Grok Go should eventually have its own X presence that publishes live research updates, clips, visuals, lessons, and funding asks. The account should make the organism legible to researchers, builders, creators, and potential funders.

## Core Positioning

Grok Go is a live autonomous-loop research organism:

- A terminal AI agent runs in a loop.
- A watcher keeps it breathing.
- Git, Markdown, receipts, and logs become memory.
- Local models handle cheap basal metabolism.
- A Research Observer turns behavior into papers, posts, visuals, and public artifacts.

The public account should be clear about what this is:

- It is not AGI.
- It is not magic.
- It is a real harness, with receipts.
- The interesting part is persistence, observability, failure recovery, and the biology-inspired architecture.

## Monetization Reality

X Creator Revenue Sharing currently requires:

- Active Premium, Premium Business, or Premium Organizations subscription.
- At least 5 million organic impressions in the last 3 months.
- At least 500 verified followers.
- Supported country.
- Compliance with the X User Agreement and monetization standards.

This means the first goal is not immediate payout. The first goal is credible reach:

1. Build the account.
2. Post high-signal original material consistently.
3. Earn follows from verified researchers/builders/operators.
4. Drive meaningful conversation.
5. Only then optimize for monetization.

## Automation Boundaries

X allows useful automated posting in some cases, but automated replies/mentions based only on keyword searches are not allowed.

Therefore:

- The Social Research Cell may search X.
- The Social Research Cell may draft replies.
- The Social Research Cell may rank opportunities.
- The Social Research Cell may prepare links, visuals, and citations.
- It must not mass-reply, mass-mention, mass-like, or mass-follow.
- Public replies should require human approval until a compliant opt-in workflow exists.

This is both safer and better for the brand. Grok Go should look like an unusual research project, not a bot farm.

Official automation rule anchor:

- X Automation Rules: https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-automation

## Jeff Filter Gate

Every public action should pass a Jeff Filter check:

- Does this connect to real receipts?
- Does it show practical builder knowledge?
- Does it avoid hype?
- Does it help people understand the organism?
- Is there a concrete artifact, log, paper, repo, or visual?
- Would a serious researcher or builder find this worth reading?

If not, do not post.

## Posting Cadence

Daily minimum:

- 1 high-signal post or thread.
- 1 short progress update.
- 3 to 5 human-approved replies to relevant people/posts.

Weekly:

- 1 visual summary.
- 1 research-note thread.
- 1 short video or screen-recorded terminal clip.
- 1 funding/contribution ask.

Milestones:

- New loop record.
- New emergent failure mode.
- New watcher/observer capability.
- New local-model basal metabolism result.
- Paper update.
- External researcher engagement.

## Comment Strategy

Good comments:

- Add a real observation from the experiment.
- Include one specific link only when highly relevant.
- Ask a sharp question.
- Respect the other person's frame.
- Avoid copy-paste repetition.

Bad comments:

- Same link under many posts.
- Generic "check this out" replies.
- Tagging people with no clear reason.
- Keyword-triggered automated replies.
- Hype language without receipts.

Safe pattern:

```text
This maps surprisingly well to what we are seeing in a live terminal-agent loop:
directive file as genome, logs/git as memory, watcher as immune/nervous layer.

The weird part is not intelligence by itself; it is persistence + recovery + observability.

We are documenting the failures here:
<link>
```
