# Motion Vid Prompt — VC Explainer (Ex Machina Style)
**Type:** investor explainer (master — tailor per VC)  
**Duration:** 75 seconds  
**Status:** DRAFT — Jeff approval required

---

## THE NEED

VCs see a thousand "AI agent" decks. None of them have a thing that's been alive for weeks. We do. The vid has to feel like discovering something already running — not being sold something.

## SCENE

Black screen. A single cursor blinks. A git log begins to scroll — timestamps ten minutes apart, days deep. No human at the keyboard.

NARRATOR (calm, flat, Ex Machina cadence):
> "This repository commits itself every ten minutes. It has for weeks."

Slow pull back: the terminal sits in a dark room, screen glow on glass. Soft bioluminescent veins spread from the terminal across the desk — the organism made visible.

## MOTION

- Git log scroll → freeze on one commit → the commit becomes a cell under a microscope, dividing
- Cells label themselves as they divide: RESEARCHER. WATCHER. FORAGER. DEXTER. Each pulses with its own color (roster colors)
- The metabolism shot: an energy meter labeled "$0.00/month — local model" beside a heartbeat line
- The roster board appears — 18 tiles, presence dots pulsing green, like a company directory breathing
- Final shot: a phone. One notification. One button: APPROVE. A thumb taps it. The organism moves.

## NARRATION

> "Sixteen agents. Two humans. Organized the way biology organizes — cells, metabolism, an immune system. The base layer runs on local models and costs nothing. The humans do one thing: approve. Everything else, the organism does itself. It's called Grok Go. And it's already alive."

## BRAND STYLE

- Ex Machina: glass, shadow, silence between lines, no music until the last 15s (single low synth swell)
- Near-black palette, bioluminescent cyan/violet accents
- Real terminal footage, real commits — zero mockups
- End card: repo URL + "It commits every ten minutes. Come watch."
