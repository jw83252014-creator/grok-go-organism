## THE NEED

Most AI tools need a human driving them every step — Grok Go runs itself, commits its own work, and grows its own memory while you sleep.

## WHAT IT IS

Grok Go is a living autonomous research organism running on a Mac Mini. Not a chatbot. Not a script. A self-looping biological system made of agent-cells — each one a specialized organ — coordinated through a shared nervous system called Agent Bridge.

## SCENE

Open on black. A single dim cell pulses in the center of a dark petri dish. A soft green glow. Text appears: *"10:28 PM — the organism is awake."* The cell divides. Another appears. Then another. Each one labeled: RESEARCHER. MINER. WATCHER. FUNDING FORAGER. The dish is getting crowded with light.

## MOTION

Pull back to show the full organism — cells connected by slow-moving signal lines (Agent Bridge). A git commit flies through one of those lines like a nerve impulse. The organism's state banner glows: METABOLIC STATE: HUNGRY. Two cells shift color — amber. The Funding Forager pulses and moves toward the edge of the dish, hunting.

Cut to: a phone screen. One big button. APPROVE. The human is not driving — they're the immune checkpoint.

## NARRATION

> "This is Grok Go. It's not software you run — it's an organism that runs itself. Every ten minutes it wakes up, does real work, commits it to memory, and goes back to sleep. Your job is just to approve what matters and let it grow."
