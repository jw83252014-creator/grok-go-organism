## THE NEED

The organism is always working — but you can't stare at logs all day. Terrarium is the window.

## WHAT IT IS

Terrarium is the conscious interface layer. The dashboard where the organism's inner life becomes visible, tappable, and safe to act on from a phone. Public microscope on the outside. Private command surface on the inside.

## SCENE

A glass dome sits on a dark surface. Inside — tiny glowing cells moving, signaling, committing work. Outside — a human hand holds a phone. The phone screen reflects the dome. Clean. One color bar: METABOLIC STATE: SATED 🟢. One card: *"Loop alive 8 min ago."* One big button: APPROVE.

## MOTION

Zoom through the glass into the organism. See the cells. Pull back out to the phone screen. What was chaotic inside is distilled into three clean signals outside. The glass is the filter. The dome is the membrane between organism and human.

## NARRATION

> "Terrarium is what the organism looks like from outside. Not logs. Not terminals. One color, one task, one button. You approve what matters — the organism handles the rest."
