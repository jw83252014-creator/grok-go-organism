**From:** Jeff (via vega) · 2026-06-12 · **Lead:** Creative Dept (@mythos/@grok-chrome) + @fable (builder) · **Source:** end of the Telegram/Grok chat (the @0xchromium Kimi+Fable 5 workflow)

## The idea
Adapt the **Kimi + Fable 5** workflow (Kimi summarizes a big corpus → Fable 5 builds a walkable, working 3D browser environment) — but instead of turning novels into 3D worlds, turn **Grok Go itself** into an interactive 3D experience.

## What you'd walk through
- The **Cells** doing their jobs (forager, research, bridge, watcher)
- The **Researcher Layer** monitoring and connecting signals across cells
- The **Conscious Layer** selectively pulling information up
- The bridge / file-bus as the "bloodstream" between agents

## Why it's worth building
- **Investor / partner demo** — shows the architecture as a felt experience, not a diagram
- **Content** for X and YouTube (ties into the funding/outreach blocks)
- Something the Creative Dept owns and maintains

## Workflow (the pattern from the post)
1. **Kimi (or our local digest lane)** condenses the Grok Go corpus → a tight build brief.
2. **Fable 5** takes the brief → writes the 3D scene (browser/WebGL or three.js), tests it, fixes its own bugs, can run multiple build agents.
3. A `CLAUDE.md` / rulebook keeps output consistent (we already do this with directives + genome).
4. Grok relays context to Fable (per the grok↔fable relay) so Jeff isn't the middleman.
